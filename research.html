Welcome to my research section, where I delve into the behavioral aspects of decision-making and social cooperation through experimental economics. To ensure full anonymity during the journals review stages preceding a publication, links to pre-registration and the files for replication are made available after publication.
Research Interests

My research interests span several fields. In behavioral and experimental economics, I focus on social norms, pro-social behaviors, risk attitudes, the civil economy, gender, and religion. In microeconomics, I am particularly interested in game theory. I also explore themes in sociology, especially economic sociology, with attention to embeddedness, social capital, and the informal economy. Finally, my work draws on psychology, engaging with economic psychology as well as the psychology of groups and organizations.
